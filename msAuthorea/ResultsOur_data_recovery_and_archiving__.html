Our data recovery and archiving efforts in context

      This effort to recover and archive data from research funded by the EVOS Trustee Council spanned ?? years, and involved ??? person hours of effort.  We were able to publish data from ?? of 315 studies; our success rate was 26% (Figure 1, Figure 2).  To put our efforts in context, we found three studies describing the success of previous efforts to recover data that was supposed to be publicly available (ex: federal and state government data, data required by journals or funders to be public).  Wolins (1962) described a rate of 24% of recovery for data from psychology studies, while 44 years later Wicherts and others (2006) reported recovering 26% of data sought in the same field.   Most recently, Savage & Vickers (2009) examined data sharing by authors of the open-access journal PLOS One, and reported recovering just 10% of the data from a set of papers published in this journal.  The results of our data archiving efforts were not significantly different than these studies (Figure 1).
